Math Vocabulary

Key algebra terms to boost your understanding

Variable

A symbol, usually a letter, that represents a number in mathematical expressions or equations.

Equation

A mathematical statement that shows two expressions are equal, using the equal sign (=).

Coefficient

The number that multiplies a variable in an algebraic expression.

Expression

A combination of numbers, variables, and operations that represents a value.

Term

A single mathematical expression that can be a number, variable, or a combination of both, multiplied together.

Exponent

The number that indicates how many times a base is multiplied by itself in an expression.

Factor

A number or expression that divides another number or expression evenly.

Constant

A fixed value that does not change.

Like Terms

Terms that have the same variable raised to the same power. They can be combined in an expression.

Polynomial

An algebraic expression made up of terms connected by addition or subtraction, with variables having non-negative integer exponents.

Distributive Property

A property that states that multiplying a number by a sum is the same as multiplying the number by each term in the sum and then adding the results.

Quadratic Equation

An equation in the form of ax¬≤ + bx + c = 0, where a, b, and c are constants, and x represents an unknown variable.

Linear Equation

An equation that makes a straight line when plotted on a graph, typically in the form of y = mx + b.

Factorization

The process of breaking down an expression or number into its factors.

Root

A solution to an equation, particularly one where the variable is raised to an exponent, or a value that when substituted into an equation makes it true.

Slope

The steepness or incline of a line, usually represented as the ratio of the vertical change to the horizontal change between two points on the line.

Intercept

The point at which a graph crosses the x-axis or y-axis. It represents the value of a function when one of the variables is zero.

Parallel Lines

Two lines in a plane that never meet or intersect, no matter how far they are extended.

Perpendicular Lines

Two lines that intersect at a right angle (90 degrees).

Absolute Value

The distance of a number from zero on the number line, without considering direction (always positive).

Factorial

The product of all positive integers from 1 to a given number (denoted by n!), used in permutations and combinations.
