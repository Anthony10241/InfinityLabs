Math Vocabulary

Key algebra terms to boost your understanding

Variable

A symbol, usually a letter, that represents a number in mathematical expressions or equations.

Equation

A mathematical statement that shows two expressions are equal, using the equal sign (=).

Coefficient

The number that multiplies a variable in an algebraic expression.

Expression

A combination of numbers, variables, and operations that represents a value.

Term

A single part of an expression, which could be a number, variable, or a combination of both.

Exponent

The number that tells how many times to multiply the base by itself.

Factor

A number or algebraic expression that divides another number or expression evenly.

Constant

A value that does not change.

Like Terms

Terms with the same variable and exponent, which can be combined.

Distributive Property

Multiplying a number across terms inside parentheses, e.g., a(b + c) = ab + ac.

Polynomial

An expression made up of terms that can include constants, variables, and exponents.

Root

A solution to an equation; for example, x where x¬≤ = 9.

Linear Equation

An equation that graphs as a straight line, often in the form y = mx + b.

Quadratic Equation

An equation in the form ax¬≤ + bx + c = 0.

Slope

The steepness of a line, calculated as rise over run.

Intercept

The point where a line crosses the x-axis or y-axis.

Absolute Value

The distance a number is from zero on a number line, always positive.

Factorial

The product of all positive integers up to a number, written as n!.
